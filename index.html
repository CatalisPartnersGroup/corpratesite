About Us

NanoFrontier, Inc. is pioneering mass production technology for functional organic nanoparticles. Our innovative detection reagents provide real-time monitoring of specific hazardous substances, with an initial focus on PFAS in water.

Founded: April 2025

Headquarters: Sendai, Miyagi, Japan

Research Facility: Tohoku University Campus

Our Technology

Real-Time PFAS Detection

Our proprietary organic nanoparticle method enables rapid, colorimetric detection of PFAS at concentrations below 100 ppt without the need for special equipment.

Scalable Production

Utilizing a unique reprecipitation process, we achieve precise nanoparticle size control, enabling mass production of high-performance detection reagents.

Our Team

Seiya Inoue

CEO

Seiya Inoue brings extensive expertise in both technology and business leadership. After pioneering research in organic compounds and protein reaction prediction at The University of Tokyo, he advanced his career at Microsoft, where he supported application developers and drove technical sales using Azure.

His entrepreneurial spirit led him to found Catalis Partners Group, focusing on AI applications and SaaS development, with a strong emphasis on organic energy materials such as amine polymers, battery electrolytes, and eco-friendly catalysts. Seiya's leadership has been recognized with 34 awards, including the JSPS Ikushi Prize (2021), the New Chemical Technology Research Encouragement Award (2023), and the Aoba Engineering Research Encouragement Award (2024).

Kouki Oka, PhD

CTO

Kouki Oka, PhD, is a leading authority in polymer chemistry and functional organic materials. His academic career includes a tenure-track position at Osaka University and a current lectureship at Tohoku University, along with affiliations at Kyoto and Waseda Universities.

His groundbreaking research spans the synthesis of functional organic materials for energy storage and conversion, as well as the study of porous materials for selective ion detection and recovery. As a JSPS Postdoctoral and International Research Fellow, his expertise in nanoparticle fabrication and precise size control through proprietary reprecipitation techniques underpins our innovative PFAS detection solutions.
